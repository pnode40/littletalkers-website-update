What is Early Intervention?

Early Intervention (EI) is Illinois' statewide program that supports children from birth to age 3 who have delays in their development — or who may be at risk for them. It's funded by the state, and every Illinois family has the right to a free evaluation.

  • ✓

    It's normal to have questions. Many parents notice something — fewer words, later steps — and simply want to understand. Asking is a sign of a caring parent, not a problem.

  • ✓

    Services happen where life happens. Therapy takes place in your home or daycare, woven into play and daily routines your child already loves.

  • ✓

    The earlier, the better. A young child's brain grows fastest before age 3 — gentle support now can make a lifelong difference.

How to Get Started in Illinois

In Illinois, Early Intervention begins with your local Child and Family Connections (CFC) office. The process is free to start, and we're happy to help you along the way.

  1. 1

    Call your local CFC office

    Anyone can make a referral — you don't need a doctor's note. Find your office at the Illinois CFC directory or call the statewide line at 1-800-323-4769.

  2. 2

    Free evaluation

    A team will play with your child and chat with you to understand how they're growing. It's relaxed, family-friendly, and at no cost to you.

  3. 3

    Build your family's plan (IFSP)

    If your child qualifies, you'll work with your CFC service coordinator to create an Individualized Family Service Plan — your goals, your routines, your child.

  4. 4

    Ask for Little Talkers

    Families can request a provider by name. Tell your service coordinator you'd like Little Talkers — then we'll come to you and begin, gently, through play.

A gentle note about waiting: Early Intervention is in high demand across Illinois, and sometimes there's a wait before services begin. Starting the CFC process early helps — and we're glad to answer questions while you wait. Reach out anytime.
